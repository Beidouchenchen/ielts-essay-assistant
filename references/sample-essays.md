Additionally, green spaces play a vital environmental role by improving air quality, providing habitats for wildlife, and mitigating the urban heat island effect.

In my opinion, the solution lies not in eliminating parks but in more thoughtful urban planning. Mixed-use developments that combine residential, commercial, and recreational spaces could reduce commuting without sacrificing greenery. Governments should also invest in efficient public transportation networks to connect outlying residential areas with city centers more effectively.

In conclusion, while the desire to reduce commuting time is understandable, destroying urban green spaces would ultimately harm the very people it aims to help. A more sustainable approach would integrate housing solutions with the preservation of nature.

### Topic: Technology - Social Media

**Prompt:** Many people believe that social networking sites have had a huge negative impact on both individuals and society. To what extent do you agree?

**[AI-Generated] Band 7 Sample:**

*Note: This is an AI-generated sample based on IELTS scoring criteria. Compare with official Cambridge IELTS samples for authentic examples.*

Social media platforms have become an integral part of modern life, with billions of users worldwide. While I acknowledge that these platforms have brought certain benefits, I strongly agree that their overall impact on individuals and society has been predominantly negative.

The effects on individuals are particularly concerning. Firstly, excessive social media use has been linked to various mental health issues, including anxiety, depression, and low self-esteem. Constant exposure to carefully curated images of others' lives creates unrealistic expectations and fosters feelings of inadequacy. Secondly, social media is highly addictive by design, with features like infinite scrolling and notifications designed to maximize engagement. This addiction can lead to decreased productivity, sleep deprivation, and reduced face-to-face social interaction. Additionally, the spread of misinformation on these platforms can mislead users and reinforce harmful beliefs.

The societal impacts are equally troubling. Social media has contributed to increased polarization by creating echo chambers where users are only exposed to viewpoints that align with their own. This has eroded civil discourse and deepened political divisions in many countries. Furthermore, the rapid spread of fake news on these platforms poses a threat to democratic processes and public health. The Cambridge Analytica scandal demonstrated how personal data from social media can be exploited to manipulate public opinion on a massive scale.

However, it would be unfair to ignore the positive aspects entirely. Social media has facilitated communication across geographical boundaries, allowed marginalized voices to be heard, and enabled social movements to organize effectively. During the COVID-19 pandemic, these platforms were crucial for maintaining social connections.

In conclusion, despite some benefits, I believe the negative impacts of social media on mental health, social cohesion, and democratic processes outweigh the positives. Greater regulation and user education are needed to mitigate these harms.

## Common Essay Types and Structures

### Opinion Essay (To what extent do you agree/disagree?)
```
Introduction: Paraphrase + thesis (clear opinion)
Body 1: Main reason + example
Body 2: Another reason + example
Conclusion: Restate opinion + summary
```

### Discussion Essay (Discuss both views)
```
Introduction: Paraphrase + thesis (mention both views)
Body 1: View 1 + support
Body 2: View 2 + support
Conclusion: Give your opinion + summary
```

### Problem-Solution Essay
```
Introduction: Paraphrase + thesis
Body 1: Problem 1 + solution 1
Body 2: Problem 2 + solution 2
Conclusion: Summary of importance
```

### Advantages-Disadvantages Essay
```
Introduction: Paraphrase + thesis
Body 1: Advantages (2 points)
Body 2: Disadvantages (2 points)
Conclusion: Balanced view/Your preference
```

### Double Question Essay
```
Introduction: Paraphrase both questions + thesis
Body 1: Answer question 1
Body 2: Answer question 2
Conclusion: Summarize both answers
```
